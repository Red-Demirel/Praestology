# Praestological Order Ontology (Working Draft 0.3)

## Preamble

This theory does not describe the emergence of existence from an absolute nothingness. 

Rather, it proceeds from the assumption that an absolute nothingness is logically excluded. Existence as such requires no origin, but develops through continuous differentiation. 

The subject of the theory is the emergence of order.

---

# Basic Assumptions

## 1. Absolute nothingness is logically excluded.
A complete non-being possesses no consistent description. The minimal consistent basis is therefore maximal indeterminacy, not emptiness. This proto-logic ("Is not not") requires no further justification.

## 2. Indeterminacy
Fundamental existence initially possesses no distinguishable structures. In particular, the following do not yet exist:
* Space
* Time
* Geometry
* Matter
* Energy
* Laws of nature
* Information

These terms only emerge through progressive differentiation.

## 3. No Preference
Within indeterminacy, no possible differentiation holds fundamental precedence. There exists neither a distinguished center nor a preferred direction or perspective.

## 4. Differentiation
Differentiation is the fundamental organizing principle. It is the continuous formation of determinacy within indeterminacy. The frequently used term **"process"** serves merely as a description of this continuous differentiation and does not represent an additional ontological entity.

## 5. Context
Persistent differentiations are integrated. The context is the interwoven fabric of all stable differentiations. It does not store the past as a chronicle, but as a currently active structure. Every new differentiation alters the entire context.

---

# Recursive Development

The theory understands reality as recursive context development.
* Differentiation generates context.
* Context alters the possibilities for further differentiation.

This creates a continuous recursive development. The process possesses no global origin and no distinguished center. Every differentiation acts exclusively relative to its local context.

---

# Cross-Level Effects

Differentiation acts simultaneously across all organizational levels. It concerns in particular:
* Stabilization of rules
* Formation of geometry
* Materialization
* Physical structures
* Biological organization
* Cognitive organization
* Consciousness

These levels represent different manifestations of the same recursive organizing principle.

---

# Laws of Nature

Laws of nature emerge as highly persistent invariants of recursive context development; they are not fundamentally presupposed. Rules are therefore a stable consequence of its development.

---

# The Quantum World and the Classical World

The quantum world and the macroscopically experienced world are different organizational states of the same differentiation process. The classical world represents a highly integrated context.

---

# Methodological Principle

Because space, time, geometry, and laws of nature are themselves considered emergent, terms from these domains can only be used analogously at the fundamental level. They serve description, not definition. The theory therefore consistently distinguishes between ontological statements and linguistic auxiliary terms.

---

# Guiding Principle

The foundation is the recursive evolution of differentiation. The observable world is the current, integrated context of this continuous development.

# EXTENDED FRAMEWORK (Integrating Deduced Mechanics)

## 6. The Mechanism of Realization ("Not-Nothing")
The transition from indeterminacy to differentiation is driven by a logical compulsion. Absolute nothingness is a paradox; therefore, the system is compelled into a state of "Not-Nothing." The fundamental event is a double negation: the rejection of non-existence. This logical resolution is the true nature of differentiation.

## 7. Adaptation Over Accumulation
Context growth is not an additive "stacking" of independent events. Because the system possesses no global coordinate system, a new differentiation can only be defined by its relationship to the existing context. Thus, integration is strictly adaptive. Each new event must structurally conform to the pre-existing relational web, which in turn subtly reshapes the entire web.

## 8. The Emergence of Time and Dynamics
* **The Future (The Probabilistic):** Represents the state of indeterminacy relative to the current context. It possesses no preference and is purely potential.
* **The Past (The Contextual Frame):** Represents the fully integrated context. Once a differentiation is realized, it becomes a persistent structural constraint — an active, shaping force on the present, subject to continuous re-integration and Regress.
* **The Now (The Realization Process):** The boundary where probabilistic potential is forced to adapt to the accumulated constraints of the context. 
* **Time:** "Time" is the *update-rate*—the metric of how rapidly successive differentiations can be integrated into the existing context.
* **Dynamics:** Motion and change are the descriptive consequence of the sequential realization of structural constraints.

## 9. Inertia as Contextual Friction
Because the past acts as structure, altering a highly integrated context requires overcoming structural resistance. This resistance is experienced as **inertia**. A dense, complex web of differentiations (e.g., "mass") presents high friction against new adaptations, inherently slowing down the update-rate. Therefore, what we perceive as time dilation in the presence of mass is the direct consequence of the realization process facing increased contextual resistance.

### Mapping to Praestology Rules (R1–R5)

# The Cycle of Realization (The R-Sequence)

To formalize the transition from the probabilistic to the static, a single realization event follows a strict, recursive sequence:

R1 (Potential): The local context presents a state of indeterminacy (No Preference) relative to its next step.
R2 (Compulsion): The logical impossibility of absolute nothingness ("Is not not") forces the resolution of this potential into a new differentiation.
R3 (Relational Integration): The new differentiation does not exist in a vacuum. It is structurally defined exclusively by its relationship to the pre-existing context (Adaptation).
R4 (Relational Constraint & Adaptive Shaping): A new differentiation must structurally conform to the pre-existing context. If fundamentally incompatible, the differentiation fails to integrate; it does not "loop back," but rather remains unresolved potential, unable to manifest as a persistent structure in that specific relational space.
R5 (Structural Fixation or Collapse): There is no active "evaluation," only structural consequence. 

    Fixation: If the integration is viable (compatible with the metastable zone of the local context), the differentiation stabilizes. It becomes a persistent structural constraint (the accumulated context), updating the relational web.
    Collapse (Evolutionary Rejection): If the integration is non-viable (incompatible with the metastable zone), the structure fails to stabilize. It is structurally nullified or immediately superseded by the surrounding context, preventing it from becoming part of the persistent frame. Unresolved aspects simply remain as indeterminacy for future adaptation.
