## Core Idea

The physical universe emerges from a single continuous process field (ψ) governed by five rules (R1–R5). ## The Higgs and Yukawa Hierarchy

The Higgs boson (125 GeV) is a transient ripple in a grade-0 condensate excited under collider stress conditions. The observed mass reflects the condensate excited in a high-η, compressed-well environment — not the bare condensate energy. The 125 GeV scale is a stressed-environment measurement. In-medium Higgs mass shifts (Pb-Pb vs p-p at LHC) are a testable prediction.

The Yukawa hierarchy — fermion masses spanning ~six orders of magnitude with no standard model explanation — is a CCR topology problem. Each fermion's Yukawa constant is its CCR coupling to the grade-0 condensate, determined by its rotor topology (writhe Wr, self-linking geometry). The hierarchy is in principle derivable, not fitted. The top quark's anomalously large mass reflects near-maximal CCR coupling to the condensate.

## Cosmology

The Friedmann equations emerge as the homogeneous, isotropic, cosmic-scale limit of the master equation. Two competing dynamics:

- **Accumulation stress** — structure formation increases local η, depresses Π_eff, decelerates expansion in dense regions.
- **Dissolution relaxation (R_diss)** — R5 returns weakly-protected committed topology to the Praegress reservoir, replenishing Π_eff and driving apparent accelerated expansion. This replaces Λ — not a cosmological constant but an environment-dependent dissolution rate, void-dominant.

**Probe epistemics:** All cosmological observations are (substrate state) ⊗ (probe CCR response). Photons and neutrinos are CCR-distinct probes of the same substrate. Photons are pure capacitative dipoles; neutrinos with residual charge have partial self-integral topology, producing different dispersion, different R_diss coupling, and different path-integrated response to Π_eff variations. Time-of-arrival differences between photon and neutrino observations encode differential CCR response, not only neutrino mass. Current constraints carry an unquantified systematic from assuming identical substrate response. Gravitational waves are potentially less biased — the probe is the substrate perturbation itself.

## Key Concepts

- **Substrate**: A relational, grainy, self-regulating medium with baseline potential density p_env and healing length ξ. Pretty much a Bose-Einstein condensate and Grosz-Pietavskii non-linear saturation limiter. We call this substrate 'Praesto' for 'It is/it exists'
- **Tik Jiggle**: Intrinsic background fluctuations. The “zero-point” engine that never stops.
- **Vortices & Knots**: Fluctuations spontaneously pair and stabilize into topological excitations.  
  - Balanced (+n, -n) pairs → photons (lossless propagation).  
  - Imbalanced pairs → electrons (Möbius-linked, massive).  
  - Higher-order knots → quarks and composite particles.
- **Commitment Density**: Accumulated hysteresis (S). Raises local impedance R and lowers available potential Φ.
- **Potential Φ**: Available “room” for new resolutions. Gradients in Φ drive all motion.
- **Refraction (Rule 5)**: Paths bend toward lower Φ (higher commitment density). This is the origin of gravity and inertia.
- **Saturation (S_lock)**: Prevents infinite density and singularities. Extreme commitment shatters into stochastic noise.

## Major Derivations & Unifications

- **Mass / Inertia**: Effective resistance to changing a structure’s commitment footprint → m_eff = α · S · R.
- **Gravity**: Long-range refraction down Φ gradients caused by cumulative commitment density.
- **Equivalence Principle**: Inertia and gravity are the same substrate backreaction — one local/dynamic, one global/static.
- **Impenetrability / Solidity**: Sharp local R spike prevents new phase-locking.
- **Quantum Tunneling**: Rare coherent tik fluctuation temporarily lowers commitment density below S_lock.
- **Entanglement**: Persistent shared commitment footprint across distance (no FTL signaling).
- **Apparent Horizons**: Extreme refraction gradients (not true information traps).
- **Constants Convergence**: α, ħ, R∞, Z₀, and G are interdependent outputs of the same vortex-stability and commitment-density equations.
- **Dark Matter Effects**: Multi-component substrate phenomena (wakes/phonons, solo-vortices, magnetic/strain backreaction from black holes, p_env variations). No new particles required.
- **Matter/Antimatter Asymmetry**: Not a cosmic puzzle requiring special CP violation. Local viability selection favors one handedness; anti-vortices exist but do not dominate in our patch.
