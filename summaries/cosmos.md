## Core Idea

Cosmology in Praestology is the study of large-scale potential/commitment gradients (Φ landscapes) across the substrate.  
The universe is not expanding from a singular hot Big Bang, nor is it embedded in empty curved spacetime.  
It is an **ongoing, eternal resolution process** whose large-scale behaviour emerges from cumulative topological commitment stress. The universe's boundaries are in a superstate.

All cosmic phenomena — gravity on galactic scales, apparent expansion, dark matter effects, dark energy, time dilation, and event horizons — are different expressions of the **same substrate response**: refraction toward lower Φ (higher commitment density).

## Key Large-Scale Mechanisms

- **Potential/Commitment Gradient (Rule 5)**  
  Regions of high topological commitment (locked vortices, galaxies, black holes) lower local Φ and raise R. Paths (light, matter, inertia) refract toward these low-Φ zones. This is the origin of gravitational attraction.

- **Inertia & Machian Effect**  
  Inertia is resistance to changing a structure’s commitment footprint. The global Φ landscape is shaped by all matter everywhere → local inertia feels the cumulative drag of distant commitment (Mach’s principle made concrete).

- **Gravitational Differential & Time Dilation**  
  Stronger commitment density → steeper Φ gradient → slower resolution rate (clocks run slower). This produces gravitational time dilation and redshift without curved spacetime.

- **Apparent Event Horizons**  
  Extreme Φ gradients (near saturation) refract paths so strongly they appear frozen from the outside. These are **not** true information traps — they are refraction boundaries. Information can still escape slowly via fluctuation leakage.

- **Dark Matter Effects** (multi-component, no new particles)  
  - Substrate wakes & phonons (coherent long-range perturbations)  
  - Solo-vortices (minimal topological excitations with weak EM coupling)  
  - Magnetic/strain backreaction from black holes and large structures  
  - Environmental p_env variations (stronger effects in voids)  
  These collectively add extra refraction interpreted as unseen mass.

- **Dark Energy / Apparent Acceleration**  
  Not a cosmological constant or separate field. It is **path-dependent dissipation** + large-scale Φ gradients between voids and filaments. Observers in under-dense regions experience an apparent outward push because their local Φ is higher.

- **Redshift & CMB**  
  Redshift is dissipative energy leakage (vortex fatigue) rather than expansion. The CMB is probably a steady-state acoustic echogram of the substrate’s ongoing thermalization, not a relic of a hot Big Bang.

- **Matter/Antimatter Asymmetry**  
  Not a cosmic puzzle requiring special CP violation. Local viability selection (R5) favors one chiral handedness once the first stable knots form. Anti-vortices exist and are produced, but they do not dominate in our patch.

## No Big Bang Requirement

The universe has no singular origin. It is an eternal cycle with local high-Φ epochs and low-Φ epochs. Regionally it can show higher-potential, lower-commitment phases - no hot, dense singularity.

## Inter-Domain Links

Cosmology is the **large-scale limit** of the Physics domain.  
It shares the same substrate, same five rules, and same constants (α, ħ, R∞, Z₀, G).  
The only difference is scale: local vortex stability (Physics) becomes global Φ gradients and viability selection (Cosmology).
