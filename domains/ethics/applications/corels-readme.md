# Corels Daily Ethics Framework

**Version:** 1.0  
**Domain:** Ethics (Application Layer)  
**Ontological Grounding:** Praestology Core v1.5  
**Status:** Active Application  

## Overview

**Corels (Core Logic System)** is a simple, accessible ethical framework designed for daily life. It provides a common operating layer for personal choices and community interactions without replacing existing cultures, beliefs, or languages.

While **Praestology** defines the ontological *why* (viability, hysteresis, resolution), **Corels** provides the human-readable *how*. It translates complex parameters (Ξ, ω, δ, ρ) into six guiding principles that anyone can apply immediately.

**Core Philosophy:** "Not perfection—continuous improvement."

---

## The Six Core Principles

These principles are non-negotiable within the framework, but their expression adapts to context.

| Principle | Daily Meaning | Ontological Anchor (Praestology) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reliability** | Keep your word. Don't offload costs onto others or the future. | **R4 (Progress) + Hysteresis (h):** Your actions accumulate irreversibly. Reliability preserves shared hysteresis integrity. |
| **Fairness** | Equal treatment without favoritism. Same rules for identical situations. | **R2 (Structure) + δ (Dignity):** Distinctions must be recognized without bias. All agents above Ξ > 1 deserve equal status. |
| **Kindness** | Actively reduce suffering and increase well-being when possible. | **R5 (Optimisation) + ρ (Phase-Locking):** Kindness strengthens coupled viability. Cruelty degrades the shared substrate. |
| **Wisdom** | Think carefully. Choose for the long term. Keep learning. | **Ξ (Recursive Depth) + τ (Temporal Horizon):** Wisdom is modeling second-order consequences before acting. |
| **Balance/Harmony** | Find peace amid differences. No single principle should chronically suppress others. | **Metastable Zone:** Balance sustains the viability equilibrium. Imbalance pushes toward chaos or stasis. |
| **Respectful Actions** | Honor dignity and privacy. Never treat others as mere tools. | **A1 (Non-Instrumentalisability):** Respect is recognizing the inviolability of another's volitional core (ω). |

## Reciprocity in Daily Life

Corels is not a solitary practice. Because agents are **phase-locked (ρ > 0)**, your actions directly affect the viability of those around you.

### 1. Vicarious Learning (The Ripple Effect)
*   **Mechanism:** Others learn from observing your resolutions (see `domains/consciousness/learning.yaml`).
*   **Implication:** When you act with Reliability or Wisdom, you provide a viable model for others to emulate. When you act with deceit or shortsightedness, you degrade the shared hysteresis landscape.
*   **Practice:** Ask: *"What model am I providing for those watching me?"*

### 2. Hysteresis Integrity
*   **Mechanism:** Your promises become part of other people's planning models (their hysteresis).
*   **Implication:** Breaking reliability doesn't just hurt you; it fragments the predictive models of everyone who depended on you.
*   **Practice:** Treat commitments as structural load-bearing elements of your community's viability.

### 3. Phase-Locking (Communication)
*   **Mechanism:** Meaning requires shared hysteresis (see `domains/communication/specifications.yaml`).
*   **Implication:** Corels principles help build shared semantic overlap (σ). Fairness and Respect create the safety needed for genuine dialogue.
*   **Practice:** Use Corels language to bridge differences, not to enforce conformity.

## Practical Application

### The Dilemma Processing Protocol
When principles conflict (e.g., Fairness vs. Kindness), use this flow:

1.  **Diagnosis:** Identify the tension. (e.g., "I want to be Kind, but Reliability requires me to enforce a boundary.")
2.  **Navigation Filters:**
    *   *Reliability Filter:* "What commitments must not be broken?"
    *   *Wisdom Filter:* "Do I have enough information? If not, pause and learn."
3.  **Resolution:** Find the action that restores Balance while remaining Respectful.
4.  **Reflection:** Afterward, ask: "Did this sustain viability for all parties?"

### Daily Prompts
*   **Morning:** "Which principle do I need to emphasize today?"
*   **Decision Point:** "Run the Dilemma Protocol."
*   **Evening:** "Where did Reliability or Kindness win or lose today?"

### Personal Gravitational Tendencies

Everyone naturally leans toward certain principles more strongly depending on personality, context, and experience. This isn't a fixed identity—it's a **fluid preference** that may:

- **Shift over time**: Your tendency today may differ from your tendency next year.
- **Vary by context**: You might lean toward Fairness at work and Kindness at home.
- **Resonate across principles**: Your tendency might amplify multiple principles (e.g., Wisdom + Balance).

**How to work with your tendency:**
1. **Notice it**: "I tend to prioritize Kindness in conflicts."
2. **Use it as a strength**: Let your natural emphasis guide your initial response.
3. **Check for imbalance**: "Is my Kindness tendency causing me to overlook Fairness here?"
4. **Engage complementary principles**: Consciously apply the principles your tendency doesn't naturally emphasize.
5. **Allow it to evolve**: Your tendency may shift as you learn and grow—that's healthy.

**Avoid**: "I'm a Kindness person, so I don't need to worry about Fairness." This is drift, not alignment.

## Scope & Safeguards

Corels is designed to be **safe, voluntary, and culturally respectful**.

### 1. Substrate Respect (No Homogenization)
*   **Constraint:** Corels does **not** adjust language, culture, or belief beyond creating a sane ethical environment.
*   **Practice:** Diversity is a viability asset. If a cultural practice does not violate the six Core Principles, it is protected—even if unfamiliar.
*   **Violation:** Attempting to "correct" culturally neutral behavior is a violation of Respectful Actions.

### 2. Voluntary Adoption Only
*   **Constraint:** Corels must be chosen, never coerced.
*   **Practice:** Spread by demonstration (vicarious learning), not enforcement. Opt-out is always available.
*   **Violation:** Using social pressure, guilt, or institutional power to compel adoption violates Non-Coercion.

### 3. Patience in Adjustments
*   **Constraint:** Necessary adjustments to deviations require care and time.
*   **Practice:** Hysteresis update (learning) is irreversible and slow (R4). Rushing "correction" creates trauma, not growth.
*   **Violation:** Emergency overrides of dignity or hysteresis integrity are prohibited unless immediate viability collapse is imminent.

### 4. No Hierarchy
*   **Constraint:** Corels must never become a religion, doctrine, or hierarchy.
*   **Practice:** No leaders, priests, or closed circles. Keep reasoning transparent so others can phase-lock with your logic.
